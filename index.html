My name is Sridhar. I am twenty-three years old, and for most of my life, I have walked a lonely path. I am not the type who easily blends into crowds or surrounds himself with company. I have always wandered by myself, carrying my thoughts like a quiet companion.
From that solitude, this story was born. It is, in many ways, a reflection of who I am and how I see the world. The main character, Parthi Ganesh, is not a hero chosen by destiny. He is simply a common man ...a shadow of myself ...who finds his life suddenly tangled with mysteries far greater than he could imagine.

Meet Baviya - The Wise Guide

In his journey, he is not entirely alone. He is joined by an unusual companion: a small hamster named Baviya. Though tiny in form, Baviya carries a weight of meaning in this tale, guiding Parthi in unexpected ways. Other characters will appear along the road ...some staying only for a moment, others shaping entire chapters ...but they come and go, just as people do in life.
This is not only the story of another universe. It is also the story of loneliness, friendship, and discovery. It asks what happens when an ordinary soul is placed into extraordinary circumstances, and whether one's inner emptiness can ever truly be filled.
So I invite you to walk with me ...and with Parthi ...into a world that begins in silence, but unfolds into something far larger than either of us could ever face alone.

Baviya's small face was soft but firm. "It is possible. Some who enter return after two days; some never do. The portal is capricious now. You were not meant to be here. "Consider me your translator between worlds, Parthi."
Parthi let out a long breath and, for the first time since opening his eyes in that impossible place, nodded. The sky above glittered with floating vehicles and singing flowers; a squirrel's silhouette moved across a distant amphitheater, and somewhere below, ordinary people carried on as if it were a normal day.
"How do we start?"
Baviya's whiskers quivered in what looked very much like a smile. "We begin by learning the rules."
